Ethics in Social Media Research: Common Concerns and Practical Considerations

Introduction

Introduction

  • Purpose of social Media Research
    • Huge Data
    • Easily Accessible
    • Time Efficient
  • Ethical Importance
    • Privacy, Inform Consent, Confidentiality, Anonnymity
  • Objectives of the paper
    • Provide guidelines to Researchers and Institutional Review Board (IRB)

Approaches

Approaches

  1. Observational Research
  2. Interactive Research
  3. Survey and Interview Research

Observational Research

Observational Research

  • Definition: Research conducted based on publicly available data.
  • Ethical Concerns
    • Human Subject Identification
    • Privacy and Accessibility
    • Analyzing Outdated Data

Interactive Research

Interactive Research

  • Definition: Research involving direct interaction, such as friending or following someone to gain access to private content.
  • Ethical Concerns
    • Inform consent
    • Researcher Identity Transparency
    • Parental Consent for Minors

Survey And Interview Research

Survey And Interview Research

  • Definition: Data collection directly through surveys or interviews via social media
  • Ethical Concerns
    • Consent Challenges without face to face interaction
    • Confidentiality Safeguard

Confidentiality Across Methods

Confidentiality Across Methods

  • Identification through direct quotes.

Identified Gaps

Identified Gaps

  • A lack of standardized ethical guidelines specifically tailored for social media research.
  • Necessity for clearer frameworks defining privacy expectations in online research.
  • Complexities of multistate and international research due to varying legal standards for consent.

Recommendations

Recommendations

  • Researchers should Prioritize
    • Transparency
    • Robust consent procedure
    • Collection data to essentials only
    • Enforce strict confidentials standards

Issues

Issues

Privacy

Privacy

  • Public vs private platform (Facebook vs Whatsapp)
  • Unreliable and ambiguous consent to terms and conditions (Facebook 14000 words)
  • Different platforms, different expectations (friend’s img)
  • Terms and services differ significantly

Anonimity

Anonimity

  • PHR requires sensitive data (age, medical history, ancestry)
  • Powerful search engines
  • Easily trackable

Authenticity

Authenticity

  • Swarm of spambots
  • AI content
  • Sanitization of data

Evolving Platforms

Evolving Platforms

  • Change in terms and conditions
  • Newer ways to connect
  • Red queen hypothesis

Suggestions

Suggestions

  • Know the platform
    • Stay up to date on the terms and services
  • Informed consent
    • Keep the participant involved as much as possible
  • Data security
    • Sensitive data requires hight level of security

Conclusion
