Ethics and Privacy in Social Media Research for Mental Health

Nicholas, Onie & Larsen (2020)

Purpose of the research

Purpose of the research

In today's world, around 4 billion people use social media platforms. People share all sorts of emotions on these platforms. These emotions can be used to provide mental care to humanity.

While serving this great cause raises several ethical concerns relating to privacy, consent, and the use of data. In this research, the authors raised concerns and suggestions that may help to make this process beneficial for human society.

Ways social media being used

Ways social media being used

  1. Risk Detection: Using machine learning algorithms to analyse certain traits, keywords, and images to detect depression and the risk of suicide.
  2. Crisis Response: Developing an alert-based indicator relying on others in close vicinity, that people you know are showing signs of distress.
  3. Peer Support: Social Media communities are being used as a place to come together and share advice, providing support, and validating their struggle.

Core Ethical Challenges

Core Ethical Challenges

  • Lack of Informed Consent: Social media platforms claiming use of user data as legal on the basis of provided terms and conditions, as well as the argument of "Legal = Ethical"
  • Risk of privacy and Re-identification: Breach of private information and re-identification of of user identifiers hamper public safety.
  • Fear of Data Misuse: This breach of data can later result in discrimination and a stigmatizing look from jobs and society.
